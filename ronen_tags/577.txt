S. Korea asked to stop China-bound missionaries .
BEIJING 1996-08-27
<LOCATION>Beijing</LOCATION> has called on Seoul to stop South Korean missionaries from travelling to China , a South Korean embassy spokesman said on Tuesday .
The appeal was made on Sunday during talks between South Korean deputy Foreign Minister <PERSON>Lee Ki</PERSON>-choo and his Chinese counterpart <PERSON>Tang Jiaxuan</PERSON> , the spokesman said .
It is not known why China raised the issue .
Atheist China officially bans missionary activities but often turns a blind eye to religious activities of people nominally employed as foreign language teachers , particularly in remote areas that are unable to attract other candidates .
